How to Play!

Objective: You must clear the water of pollution and increase the amount of oysters in the water.

  • Oysters and garbage will appear on your screen; click on the garbage before it disappears.
  • Clicking on an oyster will decrease your score.
  • Under a time restraint, you must filter a set amount of water.
  • The rate of disappearance will gradually increase as the levels increase.
  • Learn about the Billion Oyster Project and help the effort!
  • Volunteer your time today!

The Billion

Oyster Project

  • In 1609, there were 220,000 acres of oyster reefs.
  • 1906-1971, oysters were completely absent from New York Harbor, and the toxicity of the water was too high for any to grow.
  • Oysters filter the water. One oyster can filter 50 gallons.
  • Currently, 11,500,000 oysters have been restored.
  • The Clean Water Act was a huge factor in improving the water quality. It made the Billion Oyster Project possible.
  • Oysters are a keystone species. Without them other organisms would not have the reef, in which they live.
  • Don't forget to volunteer!
